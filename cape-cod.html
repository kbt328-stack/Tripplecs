01 · The Concept

Not A Restaurant

Triple C's Cape Cod is a mobile, event-driven, premium-priced extension of the brand designed to accomplish three strategic objectives simultaneously.

Objective One

Fund Growth

Generate substantial four-month profit to fund the build-out of Locations 2 & 3 without external capital or equity dilution.

Objective Two

Prove It Travels

Prove the brand commands premium pricing and demand outside its home market — the single most important data point for future investors and franchisees.

Objective Three

Build The Network

Build a wealthy, geographically diverse customer database in Northeast metros that informs Boston, NY, and CT market selection.

Why catering & pop-ups, not a restaurant? Prime-season Cape leases run $15K–$30K/month with year-round commitment. Board of Health permits for commercial smokers take 90+ days. Zoning restrictions on outdoor cooking are aggressively enforced. The catering and pop-up model sidesteps all of those constraints — mobile, higher revenue per engagement, fraction of restaurant capital, and downside contained to a single season.
02 · Three Revenue Streams

Each With A Job

Stream 1

Private Events & Weddings

The Cape's wedding market generates an estimated $500M in annual catering spend, with budgets from $25K to $75K per event. Triple C's competes on quality and brand story while offering a more distinctive product than standard New England seafood.

Avg ticket: $8,000–$35,000 · Target: 12–20 events · Revenue: $150K–$400K · Margin: 35–45%

Stream 2

Farmers markets in Falmouth, Chatham, Orleans, Wellfleet, Provincetown. Brewery tap-takeovers with Cape Cod Beer, Devil's Purse, Naukabout, Hog Island. Vineyard concerts, beach-club events, polo matches, yacht clubs, Oysterfest weekend.

Avg gross: $2,500–$8,000 · Target: 25–40 pop-ups · Revenue: $80K–$200K · Margin: 25–35%

Daily and weekly business of Cape Cod summer: rental property owners, real estate firms, corporate offices, concierge services for high-end vacation rentals. Lower-touch, repeatable orders with standardized menus.

05 · Operational Plan

How It Runs

Base of Operations

Commissary Partnership

Triple C's Cape Cod operates from a licensed commercial kitchen rented hourly or daily. This eliminates the need to lease a dedicated kitchen. Candidates include Cape Cod Commercial Kitchen (Yarmouth), restaurant off-hours partnerships, and church/community center commercial kitchens.

Staffing Model

Lean Is Essential

Operations Lead (Kyle): strategic oversight, sales, client relationships, marketing, large events. Pitmaster (1–2 hires, May–Columbus Day): smoker operations, prep, quality control. Event servers (per booking via agency or local network). Scott visits for largest events only as celebrity pitmaster; he does not relocate.

Quality Control

Three Firewalls

  • All recipes and processes documented during Phase 1 serve as the operating reference
  • Pitmaster hired March–April, trained at home location for 4–6 weeks before Cape launch
  • Scott reviews all event execution photos and customer feedback weekly with authority to call out quality issues immediately

Smoker Storage

Powered & Fire-Code Compliant

Smoker trailer requires secure, powered, fire-code-compliant overnight location. Trailer runs unattended for 8–14 hour briskets and 12+ hour pork shoulders. Target arrangement: farm property, marine yard, or commercial storage with 30A–50A electrical service. $500–$1,200/month.

06 · Sales & Marketing

The Pipeline Is Won Now

Cape Cod wedding and event bookings happen 6–12 months in advance. June, July, and August events are largely booked by March.

  • Build relationships with 25 Cape wedding planners and venue coordinators
  • Outreach to Cape Cod wedding venues for preferred-vendor inquiries
  • Listings on The Knot, WeddingWire, Cape Cod wedding directories
  • Direct outreach to Cape real estate firms and rental concierge services for B2B catering
  • Partnership conversations with breweries, vineyards, and venues for pop-ups
Brand positioning: authentic craft barbecue from a beloved Massachusetts pitmaster, available on the Cape for one summer only. The scarcity and story create demand. By season's end, a database of 1,500–3,000 high-value Northeast metro contacts — a strategic asset for future franchise market selection.
07 · Risk Assessment

What Could Go Wrong

Risk

Pipeline Falls Short

Minimum 6 private events booked by April 15 as go/no-go threshold. If pipeline is weak, pivot to pop-up-heavy model with lower capital commitment.

Risk

Weather Disruption

Cancellation and rescheduling clauses in all contracts. Event-cancellation insurance for high-ticket weddings. Diversify revenue so any single weather event is manageable.

Risk

Permitting Delays

Initiate permitting by Feb 1, 2026 at latest. Engage Cape Cod-experienced food service attorney. Backup plans for any town where permits are denied.

Risk

Quality Drift

Scott retains absolute quality authority. Any execution failing brand standards triggers immediate operational review and potential shutdown of further events.

Risk

Operations Lead Burnout

Hire pitmaster early and delegate aggressively. Build weekly cadence for rest. Pre-schedule one weekend per month off-Cape. Sprint, not marathon.

Risk

Capital Loss

Equipment retains 60–70% resale value. Worst-case full-loss scenario: $30K–$50K — meaningful but recoverable for a business of Triple C's scale.

09 · Strategic Value

Beyond The P&L

If this proposal were only about generating four months of catering revenue, it would not justify the operational complexity. The strategic value extends far beyond the cash.

For Investors

Brand Evidence

Every serious investor asks: "Does this concept work outside the founder's home market?" After Cape Cod: "Yes — we ran a profitable summer 100+ miles from home base, generated $400K–$700K in revenue, served 1,500+ customers across a wealthy and demanding demographic, and built relationships with the wedding and event industry on the Cape. The brand and product travel."

For Franchisees

Catering Channel Proof

Most franchise restaurant systems ignore catering as a revenue category. Cape Cod proves Triple C's can build a sophisticated catering and events business — a material franchise-system differentiator that dramatically increases franchisee AUV potential.

For Market Selection

The Database

1,500–3,000 high-value customers from Boston, NYC, CT, Northern NJ segmented by event type, ticket size, and origin. Directly informs where to open Locations 2–5 and which markets to prioritize for franchise development.

For The Brand

Content Library

A summer of weddings, pop-ups, and high-quality content creates a marketing asset library worth tens of thousands of dollars. Future franchise development, social media, PR pitches — all leverage Cape Cod content for years.

The bottom line. Cape Cod summer 2026 is not a side project. It is a deliberately structured four-month sprint that funds the next phase of Triple C's growth, proves the brand travels outside its home market, builds the customer database that informs future market selection, and creates the brand evidence that makes franchise and investor conversations dramatically more productive. The cash is meaningful. The strategic value is transformative.
