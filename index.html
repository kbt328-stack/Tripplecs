Executive Summary

A Brand In Disguise

Triple C's is not a restaurant. It is a brand in disguise — demonstrating three of the four conditions of a nationally franchisable concept. The fourth, operational repeatability, is what this roadmap exists to build.

What started as a single barbecue operation has, through Scott's craft and consistency, demonstrated a distinctive product the market is willing to wait in line for, demand that consistently exceeds supply, and a price-to-experience ratio that drives repeat visits and word-of-mouth without any paid marketing infrastructure.

The path forward is not to work harder. It is to systematize what already works, prove it travels, and build a franchise system that compensates Scott for the brand he has built rather than the hours he personally cooks.

Two Paths Forward

Stay The Course, Or Build The System

Both paths start in the same place. They diverge based on the decisions made over the next twelve months.

Path A: Stay The Course

  • Single-location operation with founder cooking daily
  • Revenue capped at current operating capacity
  • Enterprise value tied to one location and Scott's presence
  • Sale price = trailing cash-flow multiplier (3–4x)
  • Estimated exit ceiling: $3M–$5M
  • Burnout risk increases as demand exceeds capacity
  • No legacy asset beyond Scott's working years

Path B: Build The System

  • Systematize, document, replicate across multiple locations
  • Corporate-owned Locations 2 through 5 over 24 months
  • Franchise system with documented operations, proven economics
  • Sale price = EBITDA multiplier (8–12x) + royalty stream
  • Estimated exit potential: $25M–$100M+
  • Scott shifts from cook to brand steward
  • Generational wealth and brand legacy

Scope of Work

The Five Pillars

The blueprint above is not theoretical. Here is the specific scope of work proposed across every phase.

Pillar One

Strategic Architecture

Designing the multi-entity legal structure with franchise counsel. Building and refining the unit economic model. Coordinating attorneys, CPAs, and consultants as central project manager. Designing franchisee qualification methodology. Leading investor conversations and exit preparation.

Pillar Two

Systems & Operations Documentation

Converting Scott's craft into transferable, scalable processes. Operations manual, recipe book, training curriculum. Technology stack for each location: POS, inventory, scheduling, training delivery, customer database. Data and reporting infrastructure that makes every location measurable.

Pillar Three

Digital & Website Infrastructure

Custom website build with mobile-first design optimized for transactions and franchise inquiries. Integrated online ordering for pickup, delivery, and catering. Customer database integration feeding segmented marketing automation. Location pages built to scale, performance infrastructure tuned to Core Web Vitals.

Pillar Four

Marketing with Extreme Analytics & SEO

Paid acquisition across Meta, Google, YouTube, TikTok. Creative production engine for photo, video, and ad copy. CRO on every funnel. Email and SMS automation with behavioral segmentation. Loyalty design. Multi-touch attribution. Real-time dashboards. Local SEO, content SEO, AI search optimization, backlink acquisition.

Pillar Five

Franchise System Development

Working alongside franchise counsel and consultants: franchise development website and lead nurturing, franchisee onboarding experience, ongoing marketing support (local launch playbooks, creative assets, corporate marketing services), and franchisee community building.

Risk & Mitigation

Five Honest Risks

Risk One

Quality Dilution

Every location built against documented standards. Every batch measured against documented specs. Phase 1 documentation is the firewall protecting the product Scott built.

Risk Two

Founder Burnout

Phase 2 explicitly reduces Scott's daily footprint as Location 2 launches. By end of Phase 3, Scott transitions from daily operator to brand steward.

Risk Three

Capital Constraints

Phase 1 financial cleanup enables SBA financing, equipment financing, and friends-and-family rounds. Capital availability scales with credibility.

Risk Four

Franchise System Failure

Controlled launch limits franchise sales to 1–3 units in year one. Franchisee qualification, training, and support over-resourced to prevent the failure modes that kill emerging brands.

Risk Five

Market Timing

Multi-year timeline provides flexibility. Expansion pace calibrates to market conditions. Unit economics built in Phases 1 & 2 are defensible in adverse markets.
