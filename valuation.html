Why The Multiplier Changes

3x To 12x. Then Add Royalties.

Today

Cash-Flow Multiple

A single-location, founder-dependent operation is valued on trailing cash flow at roughly 3–4x. The acquirer is buying a job. Sale price is bounded by what the owner personally produces.

Ceiling: $3M–$5M.

Year 5

EBITDA Multiple + Royalties

A multi-unit franchise system with documented operations, predictable unit economics, and a recurring royalty stream is valued at 8–12x EBITDA on the corporate side plus a separate multiple on the royalty stream.

Potential: $25M–$100M+.

Why Barbecue Wins

A Structural Moment

Category

White Space at Premium

National chains compete on price and convenience, leaving the craft-quality segment underserved at scale. The premium segment is wide open.

Storytelling

Regional Identities

Built-in brand storytelling, marketing differentiation, and protectable positioning. Texas brisket, Carolina chopped, Memphis ribs — the category trades on origin.

Margins

Smoke Beats Fry

Smoked product margins, when properly costed and systematized, are among the strongest in casual dining.

Catering

Second Revenue Channel

High-ticket events provide a secondary revenue channel that most quick-service concepts cannot match. Multiplies AUV.

Franchisees

Owner-Operator Fit

Barbecue attracts owner-operators willing to invest in craft, which produces better unit-level execution than purely transactional franchise models.

Catalyst

The Window Is Now

The optimal moment to build a national barbecue brand is when the operation has proven product-market fit but has not yet been replicated. That window is open for the next 12–24 months.

The Investor View

Five Locations Is The Threshold

Phase 4 opens Location 5 — the typical threshold at which serious institutional investors begin paying attention to emerging franchise systems. With five corporate-owned locations operating profitably and the first 1–3 franchises operating, the business has the data and the proof needed for real conversations with private equity firms and strategic acquirers.

Position is not for sale. Position is evaluating partnership and capital options to accelerate growth on favorable terms.

Exit Pathway 1

PE Recapitalization

Sell a majority stake to a restaurant-focused PE firm while retaining ongoing equity and operational role. Partial liquidity now, a second exit later at potentially higher multiples.

Exit Pathway 2

Strategic Acquisition

Sale to a multi-brand restaurant holding company (Inspire Brands, Sun Holdings, category-specific consolidators) at premium multiples for established franchise brands.

Exit Pathway 3

Continued Private Ownership

Retain the asset and operate it for ongoing royalty income. Build generational wealth without an exit event.

Exit Pathway 4

Public Market

Less common but available at sufficient scale, through reverse merger or direct listing.
